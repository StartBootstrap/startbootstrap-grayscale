Helping people save money

Target 1/3

How might we help people save money — for things they usually borrow for?

Obvious solution for savings now are robo advisers. The problem is, they still don't work. What does, is traditional, empathetic, realistic, face 2 face human advisers. So instead of making another Nutmeg or Betterment, we decided to improve the rocky parts of client–adviser relationship.

We did that by simplifying discovery of client's goals for the adviser. Necessary paperwork was also improved, by making it (finally) digital and easy to use. Because the best time to start saving was yesterday.

Why did we not make another robo advisor? We think the Czech market is not ready for it yet. Financial literacy and technology adoption rates are low. There already are robo advisers available, for those who can use them. Who really needs help, are those who cannot, the uneducated and less fortunate. They are more often in debt or under distraint.

My role 2/3

Find out how do (human) advisers work now:

  • research relationships and norms with Desk Research and Evidence Gathering throught the team
  • plan and conduct Exploratory Interviews with established professionals
  • create Customer Experience Map
  • plan Service Blueprinting
  • verify the synthesis with further series of Exploratory Interviews; first with advisers, then brokerpool stakeholders and finally clients

Artifacts 3/3

  • Hi-fi interactive prototype with Wireflows of the new Goal–Based section of the Investment Calculator (InKalkulačka: Podle cíle)
  • Priority guides and Wirefows for the Investment Questionnaire
  • Hi-fi interactive prototype of both print and online versions of the simplified Investment Questionnaire
  • Design elements inventory, to be made into a full Design system at a later date
  • html form elements inventory
  • Usability testing plan, A/B testing plan

ngo project needed a website

Problem 1/4

How might we explain the project's goal to help it reach public officials?

Barriers 2/4

A friend of mine who's a social anthropologist asked me to make her a website for this project she's been working on for past few years. The project has a lot of audio & video material and a clear purpose, but lacks any kind of digital presence. It's usually presented at public events and exhibitions with follow–ups on Facebook.
The project has a very limited budget.

Process 3/4

  1. Review of options to publish and maintain a simple single–page website.
  2. Rapid prototyping, peer critique and 5–second tests.
  3. Website prototype in code.
  4. Release, more testing and evaluation.
  5. Ongoing iteration and promotion.

Result 4/4

According to Google Analytics, the website was visited by every other visitor of the exphibition it was first mentioned on. It has reached about 5× as much supporters through Facebook.

The project requires minimal maintenance and the simplicity of it has inspired the researcher herself to learn to code, so she can take care of it in the future.

Old enterprise sw got bloated, so we redesigned it

Problem 1/4

How can we redesign heo for managers, so they are incentived to lead their companies more effectively?
&
How can we redesign heo for operators to perform their daily routines more effectively?

Process of redesigning one of the interface elements

Barriers 2/4

The goal of this project was to redesign an erp software package to make it attractive to potential customers, while making it easy to use for current users. While the application is robust and reliable, its interface has become bloated and hard to use over the years.
Another challenge was that on this redesign a much bigger design change, in the corporation itself, was to be evaluated. So the project team had to take into account the whole portfolio, which consisted of a bunch of different software suites.

Process 3/4

  1. Stakeholder Analysis
  2. Business Model Canvas
  3. Target Audience Identification
  4. Partnership Model
  5. Usability Field Studies
  6. Usability Testing with 6 humans
  7. Co–design workshops with corporate stakeholders
  8. Heuristic Evaluation
  9. Prototyping
  10. Wireflows

Result 4/4

The obvious solution was (Microsoft Office's) the Ribbon. So at the agency we conducted research, designed prototypes and tested it all. We also added the Dashboard to provide an overview of the most important views each user needs.
